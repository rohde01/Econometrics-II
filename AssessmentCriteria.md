# Econometrics II | Department of Economics, Copenhagen
## Assessment Criteria

---

### Writing and Structure

The assignment must be written in a formal and precise language with an academic structure. The assignment must be readable on its own. In particular, it should be clear which question the assignment addresses, how it is done, and what the conclusion is.

---

### Econometric Theory

The section on econometric theory must include the following:

1. A precise definition and interpretation of the model considered and its properties.
2. A precise description of the estimator used. In particular, a precise account for the assumptions used to derive the estimator must be given.
3. A precise presentation of the sufficient assumptions for consistent estimation and valid inference. This includes a precise presentation of the null hypotheses, test statistics, and asymptotic distributions used to test relevant hypotheses.
4. The theory must be presented in a logical order with a correct and consistent use of notation.

---

### Presentation of the Data

Graphs of the data and relevant transformations must be presented. The data source must be mentioned.

---

### Empirical Analysis

The empirical analysis must include the following:

1. A presentation and discussion of the relevant empirical results, so that the reader is able to understand and reproduce the steps carried out in the process as well as the conclusions made. The results must be presented in suitably formatted tables. Irrelevant information should be left out of the tables.
2. A description of the model selection process based on a general-to-specific approach, information criteria, or both.
3. A discussion of whether the assumptions for consistent estimation and valid inference are satisfied for the estimated models. Specifically, this includes misspecification testing, which must be presented and discussed before statistical testing is carried out.
4. A clear conclusion to the main question and a discussion of the limitations of the approach used to reach the conclusion.
