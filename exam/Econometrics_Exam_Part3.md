## Part 3 — The Additional Assignment

Provide rigorous derivations and explanations to the following questions.

### Setup

Consider a bivariate VAR(1) model for $Z_t = (y_t, x_t)' \in \mathbb{R}^2$, given by:

$$Z_t = \Phi Z_{t-1} + \varepsilon_t, \quad t = 1, 2, \ldots, T, \tag{5.1}$$

with $Z_0$ given, and

$$\Phi = \begin{pmatrix} \phi_1 & \phi_2 \\ 0 & \phi_3 \end{pmatrix}, \tag{5.2}$$

where $\phi_1, \phi_2, \phi_3 \in \mathbb{R}$.

The process $\{\varepsilon_t\}_{t=1}^{T}$ is i.i.d., independent of $Z_0$, and satisfies:

$$\varepsilon_t = \begin{pmatrix} \varepsilon_{1,t} \\ \varepsilon_{2,t} \end{pmatrix} \stackrel{d}{=} N(0, \Sigma), \quad \Sigma = \begin{pmatrix} \omega_{11} & \omega_{12} \\ \omega_{12} & \omega_{22} \end{pmatrix}, \quad \text{positive definite} \tag{5.3}$$

**Information Set:** $\mathcal{I}_t = \{Z_0, Z_1, \ldots, Z_t\}$ denotes all information available at time $t$ (past and current values of observed series).

Conditional expectations $E[\cdot | \mathcal{I}_t]$ are interpreted as expectations conditional on this information.

**Model Parameters:** $\theta = (\phi_1, \phi_2, \phi_3, \omega_{11}, \omega_{12}, \omega_{22})'$

**Stationarity Assumptions:** $|\phi_1| < 1$ and $|\phi_3| < 1$ throughout.

---

### Question (1)

The reduced-form impulse-response function is given by the elements of $\Phi^k$, $k = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$

**(a)** Show that, for $\phi_1 \neq \phi_3$, the (1,2)-element of $\Phi^k$, measuring the response of $y_{t+k}$ to a unit shock in $\varepsilon_{2,t}$, is:

$$\Phi^{(k)}_{12} = \phi_2 \cdot \frac{\phi_1^k - \phi_3^k}{\phi_1 - \phi_3}, \quad k \geq 1 \tag{5.4}$$

**(b)** Derive $\Phi^{(k)}_{12}$ for the special case $\phi_1 = \phi_3$.

**(c)** For $\phi_1 \neq \phi_3$, derive a closed-form expression for the cumulated impulse response:

$$\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \Phi^{(k)}_{12}$$

---

### Question (2)

Suppose that $\{\varepsilon_t\}_{t=1}^{T}$ is not i.i.d., but instead of the form:

$$\varepsilon_t = H_t^{1/2} u_t$$

where $\{u_t\}_{t=1}^{T}$ is i.i.d. with mean zero and covariance matrix $I_2$ (the $2 \times 2$ identity matrix), and $H_t$ is a positive-definite conditional covariance matrix determined by the information set $\mathcal{I}_{t-1}$.

Assume that, under this data-generating process (DGP), the process $\{\varepsilon_t\}_{t=1}^{T}$ remains stationary.

**Question:** Is the equation-by-equation OLS estimator of $\Phi$ consistent in this setting? Argue why or why not.

---

### Conditional Heteroskedasticity

We now consider a modification of the model in (5.1)–(5.3) that allows for conditional heteroskedasticity, and in particular allows the conditional variance of $y_t$ to depend on $x_t$.

Specifically, consider:

$$y_t = \phi_1 y_{t-1} + \phi_2 x_{t-1} + \varepsilon_t, \quad \varepsilon_t = \sigma_t z_t, \tag{5.5}$$

$$x_t = \phi_3 x_{t-1} + \eta_t, \tag{5.6}$$

for $t = 1, 2, \ldots, T$, with $y_0$ and $x_0$ given, and where:

$$\sigma_t^2 = \omega + \alpha \varepsilon_{t-1}^2 + \gamma x_{t-1}^2. \tag{5.7}$$

The processes $\{z_t\}_{t=1}^{T}$ and $\{\eta_t\}_{t=1}^{T}$ are mutually independent i.i.d. processes with:

$$z_t \stackrel{d}{=} N(0, 1) \quad \text{and} \quad \eta_t \stackrel{d}{=} N(0, \sigma_\eta^2), \quad \sigma_\eta^2 > 0$$

**Model Parameters:**

$$\psi = (\phi_1, \phi_2, \phi_3, \omega, \alpha, \gamma, \sigma_\eta^2)'$$

with $\phi_1, \phi_2, \phi_3 \in \mathbb{R}$, $\omega > 0$, $\alpha \geq 0$, $\gamma \geq 0$.

---

### Question (3)

Assuming a stationary solution $\{(y_t, x_t, \varepsilon_t)\}_{t=1}^{T}$ exists, show that the unconditional variance of $\varepsilon_t$ is:

$$E[\varepsilon_t^2] = \frac{\omega + \gamma \sigma_\eta^2 / (1 - \phi_3^2)}{1 - \alpha}, \tag{5.8}$$

State the conditions on $\psi$ under which (5.8) is well-defined and strictly positive.

Briefly comment on how (5.8) compares with the unconditional variance of a standard ARCH(1) model (i.e., the case $\gamma = 0$).

---

### Question (4)

Derive the conditional joint density $f(y_t, x_t | \mathcal{I}_{t-1})$ and derive the log-likelihood function of the full model.

Discuss whether the parameters of the y-equation (5.5) can be estimated separately from those of the x-equation (5.6).

**Hint:** Use the independence of $\{z_t\}$ and $\{\eta_t\}$ to factorize the conditional joint density as:

$$f(y_t, x_t | \mathcal{I}_{t-1}) = f(y_t | \mathcal{I}_{t-1}) \cdot f(x_t | \mathcal{I}_{t-1})$$

Provide expressions for $f(y_t | \mathcal{I}_{t-1})$ and $f(x_t | \mathcal{I}_{t-1})$.

---

### Question (5)

Consider forecasting the conditional variance. Define:

$$\sigma_{T+h|T}^2 = E[\sigma_{T+h}^2 | \mathcal{I}_T], \quad h = 1, 2, \ldots$$

**(a)** Derive $\sigma_{T+1|T}^2$ and $\sigma_{T+2|T}^2$ explicitly.

*(For the latter, you will need $E[x_{T+1}^2 | \mathcal{I}_T]$.)*

**(b)** Argue that $\sigma_{T+h|T}^2 \to E[\varepsilon_t^2]$ as $h \to \infty$, with $E[\varepsilon_t^2]$ as in (5.8).

---

### Question (6)

Suppose now that $z_t$ is not Gaussian, but is instead an i.i.d. process satisfying only:

$$E[z_t] = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad E[z_t^2] = 1$$

with no further restriction on its distribution. The process $\{\eta_t\}_{t=1}^{T}$ remains i.i.d. Gaussian.

Show that, evaluated at the true parameter $\psi_0$:

$$E[\varepsilon_t | \mathcal{I}_{t-1}] = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad E[\varepsilon_t^2 - \sigma_t^2 | \mathcal{I}_{t-1}] = 0, \tag{5.9}$$

Use the conditional moment restrictions in (5.9) to propose a GMM estimator for the parameters $(\phi_1, \phi_2, \omega, \alpha, \gamma)$ of the y-equation.

---

### Question (7)

Suppose that the GMM estimator $\hat{\psi}$ proposed in (6) is, when centered at the true parameter $\psi_0$, asymptotically normal (under suitable regularity conditions).

Explain how this can be used to test the hypothesis $H_0: \gamma = 0$.
