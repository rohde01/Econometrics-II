## The Exam

The exam is a portfolio exam with three parts.

### Parts 1–2: Two of the assignments from the semester
- We, the teachers, select the two assignments that are part of the exam, and they are the same for all students.

### Part 3: A new "Assignment"

---

## Parts 1–2

- We do not change any content or any data in the two assignments in Parts 1–2.
- The academic requirements for each of the assignments are not changed. For example, if Assignment 1 on forecasting with ARMA models is part of the exam, you are not required to do unit root testing.
- We only assess the final version you hand in. Use the peer feedback you have received to improve the two assignments in Parts 1–2.
- **In assessing Parts 1–2, we take into account that you have worked on the assignments through the semester, that you have read other students' assignments, and that you have received feedback. Hence, you do not necessarily obtain a top grade if you carry out the empirical analysis correctly and reach the "right conclusion". We do also base our grade on how well the theory and the thought process are described. (See the assessment criteria.)**

---

## Part 3

- This part typically focuses on theory and methods.
- Data available in Digital Exam.
- The questions are specific and do not attempt to trick you.
- You are expected to write some pages with derivations, comments, and interpretations.
- Questions cover the curriculum broadly: There will be questions on the parts of the course not covered by the two assignments selected for the exam.
- We only ask questions about what you have seen in the course (in a broad sense).

---

## Proofread!

- Allocate some time for proofreading.
- Make your statements as precise as possible.
- Read "every paragraph" carefully, and decide/argue if your statements make sense.
- Your statements should not be "approximately correct" — they have to be correct.
- Make use of logical and consistent notation.
- Get rid of typos.
